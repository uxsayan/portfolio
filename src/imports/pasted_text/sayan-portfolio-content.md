## PROJECTS (7)

### 1. Spacio | Mental Health Community — App & Café
**Category:** UX Design · **Period:** Late 2022 · **Team:** Individual

**Overview:** A mental health community space explored through both digital and physical design. Sayan conducted field research and interviews with counsellors in Pune to understand how people seek support, then designed a companion app and a physical café concept side by side.

**Process:** Research → Define → Ideate → Design → Prototype & Test

**Highlights:**
- **Spacio | Café & App** — Designed with a deliberately soft, empathetic tone given the sensitivity of the subject: soothing palettes, comforting messaging, a digital space meant to feel like warmth rather than utility.
- **Spacio | The App** — Core feature is live sessions with therapists/counsellors, progress tracking, doctor recommendations, plus supporting tools like guided breathing, soothing music, and sleep aids.
- **Spacio | The Café** — A physical mental-health community space for group therapy (music/creative sessions), with a discreet, direct-entry consulting room so individual therapy visits stay private. Designed to reduce stigma and encourage people to actually walk in.

### 2. Mumbai's Public Transport | System Design
**Category:** UX Design · **Period:** Ongoing · **Team:** Individual

**Overview:** A systems-level study of Mumbai's public transport — buses, metro, and locals — mapping the pain points commuters and stakeholders face, with the goal of identifying where UX methodology can meaningfully intervene.

**Process:** Research → Define → Ideate → Design → Prototype

**Highlights:** Three visual explorations of Mumbai's transit "lifelines" (bus, metro, local train), each framed as a poetic/lyrical reflection on how each mode weaves into the city's daily rhythm and identity — used as a narrative device to humanize the systems research.

### 3. Enfos | In-Flight VR Entertainment
**Category:** UX Design · **Period:** Early 2023 (7-week project) · **Team:** Individual

**Overview:** Integrates VR into air travel so passengers can experience a destination's culture, architecture, and history before they land — reframing the flight itself as part of the journey rather than dead time.

**Process:** Research → Define → Ideate → Design → Prototype

**Highlights:**
- **The Research** — Focused on in-flight entertainment pain points: weak content options, poor connectivity, comfort limitations.
- **Enfos (the system)** — An immersive in-flight entertainment system letting travelers explore local traditions, cuisine, and landmarks before arrival.
- **The In-Flight Arrangement** — A Blender-modeled premium-class cabin scene visualizing the full VR experience in context — local delicacies, customs, and regional detail woven into the immersive session.

### 7. A Fishy Story | World Building (Speculative Design)
**Category:** UX Design · **Period:** September 2023 · **Team:** Individual

**Overview:** A speculative design exercise exploring narrative and world-building — a fictional three-chapter story ("Fishtopia") about fish achieving global dominance, visualized with AI-generated imagery. Built to explore how speculation shapes design perception.

**Process:** Plot Building → Ideate → Visualising → Design

**Highlights:** Three chapters — discovery of walking fish in the Bay of Bengal → the hidden Fish Kingdom in the Mariana Trench → escalating human-fish conflict shaped by real-world nuclear history. A pure creative/conceptual piece, distinct in tone from the UX case studies.
